Why do you need a product development process? Quite simply it controls the costs and time taken to develop a new product. More importantly, it helps your organisation to look on product development as investment rather than risk. When you examine companies that succeed in product development (3M, Sony, Black & Decker, Polaroid) they have one thing in common, the development is divided into a number of stages. Because this article discusses products in their widest sense, covering not only hard products but services as well, we have created an idealised set of stages that should be applicable no matter your industry or the size of your project.
